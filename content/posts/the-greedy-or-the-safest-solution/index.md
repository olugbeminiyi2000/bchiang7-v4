For a long time I thought greedy algorithms were something you memorize and apply. The truth is harder and more interesting: there is no universal greedy algorithm for a new problem. You have to invent a promising first choice, then rigorously prove it never leads to a suboptimal global solution. This post walks through four problems — Birthday Celebration, Fractional Knapsack, Car Fueling, and Maximum Number of Prizes — including the wrong intuitions I had first, the bugs stress testing exposed, and what finally worked.
