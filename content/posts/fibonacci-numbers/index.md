The 100th Fibonacci number has 21 digits. Summing the entire sequence up to that point the naive way is genuinely painful, and the bigger n gets, the worse it becomes. This post traces four iterations of the same algorithm, starting from simple iteration, through the Fibonacci magic matrix, into Base 2 exponentiation, arriving at O(log N) operations that double progress with each step rather than increment it by one.
